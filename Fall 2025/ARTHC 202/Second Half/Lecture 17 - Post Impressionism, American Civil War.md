### Expatriot Artists
#### John Sargent, Daughters of Edward Darley Boit, 1888
- Technically an American citizen but never actually lived in the US
	- Father was a wealthy doctor
	- Older sibling died and his mother fell in a deep depression
		- Decided to move to Europe
	- Educated by a French painter
	- Paints portraits for the wealthy elites
- Asymmetric composition
- The daughters are arranged according to their age and represents their age by how they would be to the viewer (captures the way children emotionally age)
	- The youngest is young enough not to be proper
	- The next oldest is old enough to need to behave. Standing, not moving
	- The other two daughters are in the darkness of the next room
		- Second Oldest is facing the audience
		- Oldest daughter faces away
